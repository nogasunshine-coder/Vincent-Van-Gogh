Part B — Reading: Van Gogh's Life

Vincent van Gogh was born in Holland in 1853. He was a very emotional person, and he did not have a lot of self-confidence. Before he became an artist, he worked in a bookstore and also as an art salesman. He then moved to Belgium to study art.

In 1886, he moved to Paris to live with his brother Theo. In Paris, his paintings started to change — the colors became brighter, and his brushstrokes became shorter and faster. But Van Gogh had a difficult personality. He had arguments with friends and did not sleep or eat well.

He moved to Arles, in the south of France, and invited his friend Paul Gauguin to come and paint with him. But they had a big fight. After the fight, Van Gogh cut off part of his own ear. He was sent to a hospital called an asylum to get help.

In May 1890, he felt better and moved to a small town near Paris. But just two months later, he shot himself and died. He was only 37 years old. During his life, he sold only one painting. Today, his paintings are worth millions of dollars.

Van Gogh's Famous Paintings

⏱ 45 minutes  |  Self-Portraits + Paintings + Tables

Part A — Van Gogh's Self-Portraits

Van Gogh loved painting portraits — pictures of people's faces. Most of all, he loved painting himself. He painted himself 37 times between 1886 and 1889. These pictures are called self-portraits.

The self-portraits are all a little different. In some, he has a beard. In some, he is clean-shaven. In one famous portrait, his head is covered with a bandage — after the fight with Gauguin, when he cut his ear.

One of his most expensive self-portraits, 'Self-Portrait Without Beard', sold for $71.5 million in 1998. It was also his last self-portrait — a birthday gift for his mother.

Van Gogh said about portrait painting: "The only thing in painting that excites me most deeply... makes me feel the infinite more than anything else."

Part B — The Starry Night and Sunflowers

The Starry Night (1889)
'The Starry Night' is one of Van Gogh's most famous paintings. He painted it in 1889, when he was living in an asylum. The painting shows a dark blue night sky with big, swirling stars. The stars look like they are moving — they glow and spin in the sky. Below the sky, there is a small, quiet village. In the front, there is a tall dark tree.

Van Gogh used thick paint and short, fast brushstrokes. This special technique gives the painting its unique, energetic style. Art historians say the painting feels 'unreal' — like a dream.

Sunflowers (1888)
Van Gogh painted two series of sunflower paintings. The first was painted in Paris in 1887. The second series was painted in Arles in 1888. In the Arles paintings, bright yellow sunflowers sit in a vase.

Van Gogh painted the sunflowers for his friend Paul Gauguin. He wanted to decorate the walls of their shared studio — the 'Yellow House'. He described the colors as 'chrome yellow and royal blue' — like a stained-glass window. He wrote: "I'd like to do a decoration for the studio. Nothing but large Sunflowers..."
